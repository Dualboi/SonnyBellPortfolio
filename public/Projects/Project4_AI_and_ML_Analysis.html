Machine Learning and Data Modelling Project

This project explores how machine learning models can be applied to Ukraine's missile attack dataset, with the aim of solving two main challenges: forecasting the number of attacks in the coming month, and identifying the most frequently targeted regions. By leveraging selected data columns, this work seeks to support humanitarian, strategic, and research-based decisions.

  • Forecasting Attacks: A time series regression model is used to predict missile and UAV attacks for the next month.
  • Identifying High-Risk Areas: A Random Forest classifier determines which regions have been most consistently targeted since the start of the conflict.
  • Real-World Impact: These insights can assist with evacuation strategies, defence reinforcement, and humanitarian aid allocation.

The models have been carefully chosen to align with the dataset structure and the nature of the problems, taking into account accuracy, interpretability, and reliability.

  • Time Series Model: Utilises date-based attack records and missile counts to uncover trends over time and make informed forecasts.
  • Advantages: Easy to understand, quick to train, and efficient when trends are present in historical data.
  • Random Forest Classifier: Handles both numerical and categorical data to classify the most targeted regions.
  • Limitations: Potential risk of overfitting due to limited data and the need for frequent dataset updates to maintain accuracy.
